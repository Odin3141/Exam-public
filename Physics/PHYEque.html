Compulsory Module

1. A bus moves from rest with a uniform acceleration of 2ms-2 for the first 10s. It then accelerates a uniform rate of 1ms-2 for another 15s. It continues at a constant speed for 70s and finally comes to rest in 20s by uniform retardation.

1I.) Draw a velocity-time graph of the motion using the information given above.
1a.) The total distance travelled

1b.) The average speed for the whole journey

1c.) Average retardation as the body is brought to rest

1d.) The maximum speed attained during the motion


2. A stone is tied to a stone 30cm long and it is whirled such that it makes 4 revolutions every 2s. If the stone weighs 300g, calculate:

2a.) The period of revolution

2b.) The frequency of revolution

2c.) The angular speed

2d.) The linear velocity

2e.) The angular acceleration


2f.) The linear acceleration

2g.) The centripetal force

2h.) The centrifugal force


3. A box of mass 10kg was at rest on a shelf of height 30m before a boy placed it on the floor and pushed it through a distance of 100m in 20s with a uniform acceleration. If the coefficient of friction between the floor and the box is 0.5, Find:

3a.) The acceleration of the box

3b.) The force that was applied on the box

3c.) The work done in moving the box

3d.) The power exerted by the boy

3e.) The potential energy of the box when it was on the shelf

3f.) The maximum kinetic energy the body possessed.

3g.) The frictional force that was preventing the box from when it was on the floor.


4. A density bottle has a mass of 20 g when empty, a mass of 80 kg when filled with water and 100 g when filled with a liquid. Find:

4a.) Its loss in weight in water

4b.) The relative density of the bottle

4c.) The relative density of the liquid
